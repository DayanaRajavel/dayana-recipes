Karuvaadu Kulambu

Karuvaadu Kulambu is a flavorful South Indian curry made with dried fish, brinjal, and a rich tamarind-based gravy. The dish gets its depth from a freshly ground spice mix of coriander, cumin, and red chilies, combined with aromatic fennel and roasted chana dal. The dried fish is fried to enhance its taste before being simmered in the tangy, spiced gravy. Coconut pieces add a subtle sweetness and richness, balancing the bold flavors. This traditional recipe pairs perfectly with steamed rice for a comforting and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • seeds 3 tsp
  • Dry red chilies 6-7
  • Cumin seeds (Jeera) 1 tsp
  • Curry leaves A few
  • Tamarind water (Puli Thanni) As needed
  • Oil For frying
  • Mustard seeds (Kadugu) 1 tsp
  • Dried fish (Karuvaadu) As needed
  • Brinjal (Kathirikai) Chopped
  • Coconut pieces Small chunks
  • Roasted chana dal (Pottu Kadala) 4-5
  • Fennel seeds (Sombu) 1 tsp (ground)

Instructions

  1. Dry roast about 3 tsp of coriander seeds, 6-7 dry red chilies, and 1 tsp of cumin seeds (jeera) along with some curry leaves. Once roasted, grind them all into a powder.
  2. Next, add tamarind water (puli thanni) to a pot and stir in the ground powder.
  3. Heat some oil in a pan, then add mustard seeds (kadugu) and curry leaves. Once they splutter, add the karuvaadu (dried fish) and fry until you get a nice aroma.
  4. Add chopped brinjal (Kathirikai) and pour in the tamarind water you prepared earlier.
  5. it cook until it’s about 75% done and you see it starting to boil.
  6. Then, add small pieces of coconut (like urandai or katti size), 4-5 roasted chana dal (pottu kadala), and sombu (fennel seeds) that you’ve ground. Mix it all into the gravy and let it cook for a bit more.

That’s it—enjoy your Karuvaadu Kulambu!
